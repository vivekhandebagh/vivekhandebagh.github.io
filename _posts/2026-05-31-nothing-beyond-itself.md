Have you seen the group of otters chase the butterfly?
We really do live in an unbelievably precious world.
All of it.
All of it is just a flickering candle.

It makes me think, what all have we done? What is left to do? 

“Oh my god, guess what I found on Instagram?? We HAVE to go and do-“


-------------------------------------------------


Somehow, waking up for nothing grander than the fragrance of a dark roast feels enough.
Something about it is inconsequential in a way that makes it feel more real than the experiences we believe are of consequence. 

However, the superficiality of an experience is probably not solely a function of the experience itself.
Rather, it is a property of the mind that meets it.

The dark roast doesn't feel superficial because its smell is not something you can photograph. 
You cannot post it, and you cannot make anyone envy you for it, as much as you subconsciously want to.
There is no easy way to extract anything from it, and so the dark roast offers nothing beyond itself.

This is then the bane of modern existence: the constant impulse that intercepts every experience before it can flow through us, and converts it into something postable and provable. And this impulse is maintained by a daily drip feed of everything you are not, everything you should be, see, eat, and have. 

Oh, and if you aren’t heeding its guidance, are you even living a life worth living?
